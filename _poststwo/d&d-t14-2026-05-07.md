# 🎯 **Design Food Delivery**

## 1️⃣ Core Framework

When discussing **Food Delivery design**, I frame it as:

1. Core flows: browse restaurant, place order, deliver order
2. Restaurant, menu, cart, and order management
3. Payment and checkout
4. Order state machine
5. Dasher / courier dispatch
6. Real-time tracking and ETA
7. Inventory, menu availability, and cancellation
8. Trade-offs: consistency vs latency vs marketplace efficiency

---

## 2️⃣ Core Requirements

---

### Functional Requirements

* User can browse nearby restaurants
* User can view menus
* User can add items to cart
* User can place an order
* Restaurant can accept or reject order
* Courier can be assigned for delivery
* User can track order status
* Support payment, refunds, tips, promotions
* Support cancellation
* Support ratings and order history

---

### Non-functional Requirements

* Low-latency browsing and checkout
* High availability
* Reliable order state management
* Accurate payment handling
* Real-time delivery tracking
* Scalable restaurant search
* Eventual consistency acceptable for tracking and analytics
* Stronger consistency needed for payment and order state

---

**👉 Interview Answer**

> A food delivery system is a marketplace connecting customers,
> restaurants, and couriers.
>
> The core flows are restaurant discovery,
> order placement,
> restaurant preparation,
> courier dispatch,
> delivery tracking,
> and payment.
>
> The main challenge is coordinating many real-time states
> while keeping order and payment correctness strong.

**👉 Interview Answer**

> I would separate restaurants, menus, carts, orders,

>
> Orders are durable transactional records,
> while order events provide an audit trail
> for debugging, customer support, and reconciliation.

# 5️⃣ Restaurant Search and Menu Serving

---

## Restaurant Search Inputs

* User location
* Restaurant location
* Restaurant availability
* Delivery radius
* Cuisine type
* Rating
* Estimated delivery time
* Promotion
* User preference

---

## Search Flow

```text
User opens app
→ Location service gets user location
→ Restaurant service finds nearby restaurants
→ Filter open/available restaurants
→ Rank by ETA, rating, relevance, fees
→ Return list
```

---

## Menu Serving

Menus should be cached because they are read-heavy.

Cache:

* Restaurant metadata
* Menu items
* Item availability
* Pricing snapshot
* Popular items

---

**👉 Interview Answer**

> Restaurant discovery is a geo-search problem.
>
> I would find restaurants within the delivery radius,
> filter by open status and availability,
> then rank by ETA, rating, relevance, fees, and promotions.
>
> Menu data is read-heavy,
> so it should be cached aggressively,
> while availability may need shorter TTLs.

# 6️⃣ Cart and Checkout

---

## Cart Responsibilities

* Store selected items
* Store item options
* Validate item availability
* Calculate subtotal
* Apply promotions
* Estimate tax, fees, tip, and delivery time

---

## Price Snapshot

When user adds item to cart,
store price snapshot.

Why?

* Menu price may change
* Checkout should be predictable
* Order history should preserve original price

---

## Checkout Validation

Before order creation:

* Validate restaurant is open
* Validate items are available
* Validate delivery address
* Recalculate final price
* Validate promo code
* Pre-authorize payment

---

**👉 Interview Answer**

> Cart is temporary,
> but checkout must revalidate everything.
>
> Before creating an order,
> I would check restaurant availability,
> menu item availability,
> delivery address,
> promotion validity,
> final price,
> and payment pre-authorization.
>
> This prevents stale cart data from creating invalid orders.

# 7️⃣ Order State Machine

---

## Common States

```text
CREATED
PAYMENT_AUTHORIZED
SENT_TO_RESTAURANT
RESTAURANT_ACCEPTED
PREPARING
READY_FOR_PICKUP
COURIER_ASSIGNED
PICKED_UP
OUT_FOR_DELIVERY
DELIVERED
CANCELLED
REFUNDED
```

---

## Why State Machine Matters

* Prevent invalid transitions
* Coordinate restaurant, courier, user, and payment
* Support retries
* Support cancellation rules
* Provide auditability

---

## Example Transitions

```text
CREATED → PAYMENT_AUTHORIZED
PAYMENT_AUTHORIZED → SENT_TO_RESTAURANT
SENT_TO_RESTAURANT → RESTAURANT_ACCEPTED
RESTAURANT_ACCEPTED → PREPARING
PREPARING → READY_FOR_PICKUP
READY_FOR_PICKUP → PICKED_UP
PICKED_UP → OUT_FOR_DELIVERY
OUT_FOR_DELIVERY → DELIVERED
```

---

**👉 Interview Answer**

> I would model the order lifecycle as a state machine.
>
> This prevents invalid transitions,
> makes retries safer,
> and coordinates restaurant preparation,
> courier delivery,
> notifications,
> and payment capture.
>
> Payment capture should happen only after the order is accepted
> or after delivery, depending on business rules.

---

# 8️⃣ Payment Flow

---

## Payment Strategy

Use:

```text
Pre-authorize at checkout
Capture after restaurant accepts or delivery completes
Refund on cancellation or failure
```

---

## Why Pre-authorize?

* Validate payment method
* Reduce failed orders
* Protect restaurant and courier workflow

---

## Payment Flow

```text
User places order
→ Payment service pre-authorizes amount
→ Order sent to restaurant
→ Restaurant accepts
→ Payment captured or held
→ Order delivered
→ Final adjustments/refunds if needed
```

---

## Correctness

Use:

* Idempotency key
* Payment state machine
* Transaction records
* Reconciliation jobs
* Provider-safe retries

---

**👉 Interview Answer**

> Payment requires strong correctness.
>
> I would pre-authorize payment during checkout,
> then capture after restaurant acceptance
> or after delivery depending on policy.
>
> All payment operations should be idempotent
> to prevent duplicate charges or refunds.

# 9️⃣ Restaurant Order Handling

---

## Restaurant Flow

```text
Order created
→ Send to restaurant tablet/POS
→ Restaurant accepts or rejects
→ Restaurant estimates preparation time
→ Kitchen prepares food
→ Marks ready for pickup
```

---

## Integration Options

* Restaurant tablet app
* POS integration
* Email / phone fallback
* Web dashboard

---

## Failure Cases

* Restaurant does not respond
* Restaurant rejects order
* Item unavailable
* Prep time changes
* Store closes unexpectedly

---

**👉 Interview Answer**

> After checkout,
> the order must be confirmed by the restaurant.
>
> The restaurant can accept, reject,
> or update preparation time.
>
> If the restaurant does not respond,
> the system should timeout,
> cancel the order,
> notify the user,
> and release or refund payment authorization.

# 🔟 Courier Dispatch

---

## Dispatch Inputs

* Restaurant location
* Customer location
* Courier location
* Courier availability
* Vehicle type
* Current courier workload
* Estimated pickup time
* Delivery ETA
* Courier acceptance probability

---

## Dispatch Flow

```text
Restaurant accepts order
→ Estimate preparation time
→ Find nearby couriers
→ Rank couriers
→ Send offer to best courier
→ Courier accepts
→ Assign courier to order
```

---

## Matching Strategy

Choose courier based on:

* ETA to restaurant
* ETA from restaurant to customer
* Courier availability
* Batch delivery opportunity
* Acceptance probability
* Fairness / utilization

---

## Avoid Double Assignment

Use atomic conditional update:

```sql
UPDATE courier
SET status = 'ASSIGNED'
WHERE courier_id = 'd123'
AND status = 'AVAILABLE';
```

---

**👉 Interview Answer**

> Courier dispatch is similar to ride matching,
> but it must also consider restaurant prep time.
>
> The best courier is not always the closest courier.
> We want the courier to arrive near the time food is ready,
> minimizing both courier wait time and food delay.
>
> Assignment must be atomic to avoid double assignment.

# 1️⃣1️⃣ ETA System

---

## ETA Types

* Restaurant prep ETA
* Courier ETA to restaurant
* Pickup ETA
* Delivery ETA to customer
* Total order ETA

---

## Inputs

* Restaurant historical prep time
* Current restaurant load
* Courier location
* Traffic
* Distance
* Weather
* Order size
* Time of day

---

## ETA Flow

```text
Estimate prep time
→ Estimate courier arrival time
→ Estimate delivery travel time
→ Combine into total ETA
→ Update ETA as state changes
```

---

**👉 Interview Answer**

> Food delivery ETA is more complex than ride-sharing ETA
> because it includes both food preparation time and delivery time.
>
> I would estimate restaurant prep time,
> courier travel time to pickup,
> and courier travel time to customer,
> then continuously update ETA as the order progresses.

## Tracking Requirements

---

## Protocol

Use:

* WebSocket

---

**👉 Interview Answer**

> For real-time delivery tracking,
> the courier app sends location updates every few seconds.
>
> The tracking service streams relevant updates to the customer.
>

# 1️⃣3️⃣ Inventory and Menu Availability

---

## Availability Challenges

* Restaurant may run out of items
* Menu may change
* Store may close
* Item options may become unavailable
* Price may change

---

## Strategies

* Cache menu with short TTL
* Revalidate at checkout
* Restaurant can mark items unavailable
* Store price snapshot in cart/order
* Use event-driven menu updates

---

**👉 Interview Answer**

> Menu and inventory data can become stale.
>
> I would cache menu data for read performance,
> but always revalidate item availability and price at checkout.
>
> The final order should store a price snapshot
> so historical orders remain consistent even if menu prices change later.

# 1️⃣6️⃣ Scaling Patterns

---

## Pattern 1: Separate Read-heavy and Transactional Paths

* Restaurant/menu browsing = read-heavy
* Order/payment = transactional

---

## Pattern 2: Geo-sharded Marketplace

Shard by:

```text
city / region / delivery zone
```

Why?

* Food delivery is local
* Restaurant and courier matching are region-based
* Better latency and fault isolation

---

## Pattern 3: Event-driven Order Pipeline

```text
order state change
→ event bus
→ notification / dispatch / analytics / payment
```

---

## Pattern 4: Cache Restaurant and Menu Data

Cache:

* Restaurant list
* Menu
* Popular items
* Ratings
* Delivery fee estimates

---

## Pattern 5: Async Analytics

Track:

* Order conversion
* Delivery time
* Prep time
* Courier utilization
* Cancellation reasons

---

**👉 Interview Answer**

> To scale food delivery,
> I would separate read-heavy restaurant browsing
> from transactional order processing.
>
> Marketplace operations like dispatch should be regionally sharded,
> because food delivery is naturally local.
>
> Order state changes should publish events
> to drive notifications, dispatch, payment, and analytics.

# 1️⃣7️⃣ Failure Handling

---

## Common Failures

* Payment pre-auth fails
* Restaurant does not respond
* Restaurant rejects order
* Courier rejects dispatch
* Courier app disconnects
* Menu data stale
* Courier location delayed
* Order state update duplicated
* Delivery delayed

---

## Strategies

* Idempotent order creation
* Payment authorization timeout
* Restaurant response timeout
* Retry dispatch with next courier
* Atomic courier assignment
* Revalidate menu at checkout
* Use order state machine
* Refund or credit user when needed
* Reconciliation jobs for payment/order mismatch

---

**👉 Interview Answer**

> The system must be resilient to many partial failures.
>
> Order creation and payment must be idempotent.
> Restaurant confirmation should have a timeout.
> Courier dispatch should retry with the next best courier.
>
> All order state transitions should go through a state machine
> to avoid invalid or duplicated updates.

# 🧠 Staff-Level Answer (Final)

---

**👉 Interview Answer (Full Version)**

> When designing a food delivery system,
> I think of it as a local three-sided marketplace
> connecting customers, restaurants, and couriers.
>
> The system has three major flows:
> restaurant discovery,
> order placement,
> and delivery fulfillment.
>
> Restaurant and menu browsing are read-heavy,
> so I would cache restaurant metadata,
> menus,
> popular items,
> and availability with short TTLs.
>
> Checkout must be more strongly validated.
> Before creating an order,
> the system should revalidate restaurant status,
> item availability,
> delivery address,
> promotion eligibility,
> final price,
> and payment pre-authorization.
>
> I would model each order as a state machine,
> with states such as created,
> payment authorized,
> sent to restaurant,
> restaurant accepted,
> preparing,
> ready for pickup,
> courier assigned,
> picked up,
> out for delivery,
> delivered,
> cancelled,
> and refunded.
>
> Courier dispatch is geo-based,
> but unlike ride-sharing,
> it must also consider restaurant preparation time.
> The best courier is not always the closest one;
> we want the courier to arrive near the time food is ready.
>
> Payment requires strong correctness,
> so I would use pre-authorization,
> capture,
> refunds,
> idempotency keys,
> and reconciliation jobs.
>
> Real-time tracking and ETA updates can be eventually consistent,
> because small delays are acceptable.
>
> I would use an event-driven architecture,
> where order state changes publish events
> to trigger notifications,
> dispatch,
> payment,
> analytics,
> and customer updates.
>
> The main trade-offs are order correctness,
> payment correctness,
> ETA accuracy,
> marketplace efficiency,
> latency,
> and operational cost.
>
> Ultimately, the goal is to reliably coordinate restaurants,
> couriers,
> and customers,
> while providing fast checkout,
> accurate ETA,
> and smooth delivery tracking.
